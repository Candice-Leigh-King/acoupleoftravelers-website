Rome, the Eternal City. Full of history, romance, and home to one of the Seven Wonders of the World. If you’re lucky enough to spend more than 3 days in this beautiful city, I recommend taking a day trip! Here are 3 day trips from Rome, that I highly recommend!

- Tivoli, IT! What a charming city. Home to Hadrian’s Villa & Villa d’Este.  Hadrian’s Villa is a UNESCO World Heritage Site comprising the ruins and archaeological remains built c. AD 120 by Roman Emperor Hadrian in Tivoli outside Rome. Villa d’Este is a symbol of the Italian Renaissance, an Italian State Museum, and UNESCO World Heritage Site! Both of these are a site to see. We took this tour personally! Our guide was very knowledgable. Tivoli also has a lot of cute shops, trattoria’s, and kind locals. I highly recommend visiting Tivoli if you have time.

- Florence is a great day trip from Rome, if you don’t have time to spend multiple days there. You will easily fall in love with the city, and probably plan a trip back to really immerse yourself. It is a quick train ride, and you can easily arrive by 9 AM and leave by 8PM, meaning you will have a full day to explore! Be sure to visit Galleria dell’Accademia to see the statue of David. Get your tickets in advance!! 

- Assisi! The city of Assisi makes for a perfect day trip, and it’s easy to get to! Many buses, trains, and tours connect the two cities. Assisi is an Umbrian medieval city. Small enough that you can wander around without getting lost, but big enough to spend an entire day. It is the birthplace of St. Francis (1181–1226), one of Italy’s patron saints.

Of course there are many different cities to travel within Italy, but be sure to give some time to the lesser known cities, as well! They are just as beautiful!
