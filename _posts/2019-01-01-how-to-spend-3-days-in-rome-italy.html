The PERFECT 3-day Rome, Italy itinerary

  Rome, Italy is A DREAM vacation! It is best known for the ancient Roman Colosseum- one of the seven wonders of the world, and for good reason! When you are in its’ presence, an overwhelming feeling of beauty, wonder, and adventure washes over you. This is the sight of so much history. Rome as a whole is filled with history, and can be overwhelming when trying to plan. That’s what I am here for! I’m going to give you the travel guide to Rome that you have been searching for.

  Imagine eating gelato at the Trevi Fountain or eating pizza with The Colosseum behind you. Not only are these instagrammable photo opportunities; they are memories you will never forget! Rome can be overwhelming.. there is just so much to see and do, but I’m here to help! Here is your 3-day Rome, Italy Travel Guide:

QUICK TIPS:

  • Go during off season! I love the fall (October). The weather is nice, and it is not so crowded!
  • Learn a few Italian phrases! Most will speak English, so don’t let this deter you. It is just always kind to learn a bit of the language of the country you are traveling to!
  • Make sure you have a travel credit card that does not charge international fees and have some euros on hand just incase!
  • WALK! This city is extremely walkable. It’s beautiful to stroll the streets and take-in the beauty.
  • Wear comfortable shoes! These sandals were perfect for all of the miles I walked! 
  • Book tickets to attractions in advance!
  • Never order a cappuccino after 11 AM!

WHERE TO STAY:

We travel using points to book hotels. We stayed at:

  • The Rome Cavalieri (click here for my honest review)
  • Hilton Garden Inn Rome Claridge (click here for my honest review)

WHAT TO DO:

Day 1: Wake up early! Don’t let your day go to waste. If you’re staying at either of the Hiltons I mentioned above, breakfast *should* be apart of your stay. Fuel up early for your long, but wonderful, day ahead! Start at Piazza Navona. I loved seeing it practically empty when starting here. If you happened to miss breakfast, have a typical Italian breakfast (cappuccino & pastry) nearby.

From here, walk to The Pantheon! Entrance is free, but lines start early. You can also book a guided tour and skip the line. I recommend this one! After this, go have an aperol spritz and some lunch!

After lunch, head to the colosseum! Book an afternoon guided tour of the colosseum, roman forum, and palatine hill! I recommend this one! 

This will take most of the afternoon. Savor every moment of seeing this history. You will learn all about the gladiators, which was quite fascinating. It is a lot of walking and standing, so be sure to wear comfortable shoes! I recommend these!

By now it will be late afternoon/early evening, so go grab some gelato! Tip: Avoid mountains of bright colors! These are not authentic and tend to have preservatives & coloring. Your best bet is choosing gelato that is inside stainless steel tubs! Don’t worry about having dessert before dinner. You’re on vacation, remember? Stroll the cobblestone streets as you enjoy your creamy gelato. Take in all of the sights.

Now, head to dinner! Be sure to do extensive research, and don’t dine at a tourist trap!

Day 2: Just like yesterday, start your day early! Fuel up, and get the day started! Head over to Vatican City! Visit the Sistine Chapel, St Peter’s Basilica, and The Vatican Museums! Some prefer a guided tour in order to skip the lines, and others prefer to do it all on their own. Either way, you need to get your tickets early! Be sure to climb to the very top of the dome, this is a work out, but it is so worth it. The views of Rome are breathtaking. We spent so much time taking it all in! If you explore Vatican City will take up most of your day, so once finished head over to the Trastevere neighborhood! It is a gem! If you choose to walk, you see so many beautiful sights. I recommend an evening food tour in the Trastevere! 

The area is lively, we experienced lovely live music in front of Fontana del Ponte Sisto! Everyone was dancing, smiling, clapping… I felt as if I was in a movie! For the food tour, we did this one!  LOVED IT! Every food item we had was delicious & the wine was too! It was a fabulous way to end our day.

Day 3: Your final day in Rome! Let’s head straight to the Trevi Fountain. Get there early or you will be in a crowd of people! Make a wish, throw your coin in, and live out your Lizzie McGuire dreams! From there, go to the secret archaeological site under the Trevi Fountain! It’s a HIDDEN GEM! The Vicus Caprarius is known as The City of Water and the entrance is right around the corner from the Trevi Fountain. Not only will you see the ancient aqueducts, but hundreds of artifacts from excavation! It cost about 4 Euro, so this makes it a very cheap thing to do in Rome!

After all of this exploring in the early morning, go grab a cappuccino & pastry! Then, walk to the Spanish steps! Wonder around the cobblestone streets and take your time!

After the Spanish Steps, go find somewhere for lunch! Remember, don’t fall for tourist spots! I recommend walking a bit away from any tourist hotspots to find the best option.

After lunch, walk to Villa Borghese. This is one of my favorite places in all of Rome! Gardens, museums, a dog park towards the back, even the zoo is connected! My most favorite moment when visiting Rome is watching all of the locals lay in fields of grass with the sun shining on their faces, books in hand, picnics everywhere. It was picture perfect. A guided tour is a must if you want to really understand all of the artwork in the Borghese Gallery. 

Also, do the boat ride at Giardino del Lago! You have to! It is so beautiful, romantic, and once you see it in person, you will feel the same way.

By now, it will be evening. Choose a cooking class & learn how to make authentic Italian pasta, pizza, or tiramisu! It’s a fun activity, you will learn a lot, and connect with locals. Plus, you’ll get a very yummy meal out of it. https://www.viator.com/tours/Rome/Pasta-and-Tiramisu-Lovers-Workshop-in-the-Heart-of-Rome/d511-21369P11?pid=P00054407&mcid=42383&medium=link&campaign=rome-itin

I hope this itinerary helps you have an amazing time in Rome! I didn’t fill it with too much, because you want time to savor the eternal city. Follow me on Tiktok or Instagram if you want to see more or have questions!
