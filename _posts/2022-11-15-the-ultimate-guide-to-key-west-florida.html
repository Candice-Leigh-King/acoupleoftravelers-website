THE ULTIMATE GUIDE TO KEY WEST, FLORIDA

  What do you think of when you think of Key West? Do you think water sports, Key Lime Pie, Or Dry Tortugas National Park? Key West is all of those things and more!

  Also known as The Conch Republic, Key West is colorful and lively! The locals are quirky and kind. There’s something for everyone here. Well, minus kids. I don’t find Key West to be a very kid-friendly place.

How to get here:

  The easiest way is flying straight into the Key West airport. This is the way I recommend to everyone, especially if you don’t have a lot of time. It’s convenient, and I always enjoy walking off the plane, straight onto the tarmac, then into the physical airport! It’s definitely a fun experience to be so up close and personal with the planes on the ground.

  Another way would be to do a Florida Keys road-trip from Miami! It’s about a 3 hour drive.. sometimes longer if traffic is backed up, but the views and stops along the way are breathtaking, and fun! We will do a post on that another day.

What To Do:

  There are so many activities in Key West. Here are my top recommendations:

  • Breakfast at Latitudes! Located on Sunset Key, this cute island has one of the best restaurants with a perfect view! You CAN’T miss this. Reservations required well in advance! After you’re done at breakfast, take a stroll around the island. It’s beautiful, and will make you wish you stayed on-site in one of their cottages.
  • Catch sunset at Mallory Square and enjoy their sunset celebration! Or head to the roof top at Bistro 245, less crowded and the most beautiful view, with a rooftop bar too!
  • Jet Ski Tour!!! There are so many options! BUT we recommend Perry Excursions located at The Perry Hotel. They have an awesome guide named Tyler who is super knowledgeable about the island.
  • Dry Tortugas National Park tour! You can private charter a catamaran or take the ferry. Either way, this must be booked in advance.
  • Cocktail class at Tiki House! It has such a fun vibe, they make ICONIC drinks, and you can take a history of the Tiki cocktail class!
  • Lagerheads Beach Bar and Watersports! The Beach has CLEAR water, no seaweed (when we were there), an on-site beach bar with drinks & yummy food (try their fish dip- perfect for the travel foodie). & it really does not get as packed as I thought it would be.  It’s a bit of a *hidden gem* in my opinion.
  • Fort Zachary Taylor State Park! Swim, grill out, and see some history!
  • Barefoot Billy’s Sushi Sunset Cruise! FRESH sushi, a fun crew, and awesome views!
  • Sunset Sail with Fury! Unlimited drinks, yummy snacks, live music, and sunset!
  • Rent a boat, kayak or paddle board!
  • Go snorkeling and see all the marine life!
  • Head to the aquarium
  • Visit the Key West Butterfly Garden
  • Find all of the 6 toed cats and learn the history of Earnest Hemingway at the Hemingway house
  • Rent a Moke and explore the island! We found a lot of hidden gems and beautiful places this way.

When to visit:

  I find that Key West is great any time of year! There are multiple events throughout the year that you wouldn’t want to miss (food festivals, speed boat races, Fantasy Fest, NYE, etc.) Or maybe you do want to miss, in order to avoid the crowds. If that’s the case, be sure to check Key West’s event calendar before booking. Winter time is considered high season, meaning prices are higher and it’s more busy than usual. Hurricane season is June-November, but hotel rates are typically cheapest then. March-May seems to be a good middle point. It’s right when high season tapers off, and before hurricane season starts.  I would check the weather, compare prices, research the levels seaweed, before booking.
